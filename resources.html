How do I start a community garden? ‚ñº

Starting a community garden involves several key steps:

  1. Gather community interest: Form a planning committee of 5-10 interested members
  2. Find suitable land: Approach your local municipality about unused public land
  3. Conduct soil testing: Contact GreenSteps for free soil testing services
  4. Secure funding: Apply for our Seed Grant program (up to R10,000)
  5. Attend training: Join our weekend workshops for hands-on setup guidance

Contact us at info@greensteps.org.za to schedule a consultation!

What vegetables grow best in South Africa? ‚ñº

Year-round vegetables: Spinach, Swiss chard, kale, lettuce

Summer crops (Oct-Feb): Tomatoes, peppers, beans, squash, cucumber

Winter crops (Apr-Aug): Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, peas, carrots

Herbs: Basil (summer), parsley, coriander, rosemary (year-round)

Download our detailed planting calendar below for specific timing by province!

How much water do vegetables need? ‚ñº

Most vegetables need 2-3cm of water per week, either from rainfall or irrigation.

Water-wise tips:

  • Water early morning or late afternoon to reduce evaporation
  • Use drip irrigation or soaker hoses for 50% water savings
  • Apply mulch (5-10cm thick) to retain soil moisture
  • Group plants by water needs
  • Install rainwater harvesting tanks (we can help!)
How do I manage pests organically? ‚ñº

Organic pest control methods:

  • Companion planting: Marigolds deter aphids; nasturtiums attract aphids away from crops
  • Neem oil spray: Effective against most soft-bodied insects
  • Soap spray: Mix 2 tablespoons dish soap per liter of water
  • Hand-picking: Remove larger pests like caterpillars by hand
  • Beneficial insects: Attract ladybugs and lacewings with flowering herbs

Join our pest management workshop to learn hands-on techniques!
