Frequently Asked Questions

How do I start a community garden in my area?

+

Starting a community garden involves several steps: First, identify suitable land and get permission from the landowner or municipality. Then, gather interested community members and contact us for a site assessment. We provide free consultations, help with soil testing, and offer starter kits for new gardens. Our team will guide you through garden layout, irrigation setup, and initial planting.

What training programs do you offer?

+

We offer monthly workshops covering organic farming techniques, composting, pest management, water conservation, and seasonal planting. All training is conducted in English, Afrikaans, isiXhosa, and isiZulu by certified instructors. We also provide specialized training for school garden programs and advanced courses for experienced gardeners.

Can schools partner with GreenSteps?

+

Yes! Our School Garden Program helps educational institutions establish gardens that serve as outdoor classrooms. We provide curriculum-aligned learning materials, teacher training, and ongoing support. Gardens can supply school feeding schemes while teaching children about nutrition, science, and environmental stewardship.
